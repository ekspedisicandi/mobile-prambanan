HISTORY TIMELINE


Ancient Mataram Kingdom or Hindu Mataram kingdom (or often also called the Medang kingdom) is the name of a kingdom that stands in Central Java in the 8th century. The king of this kingdom left a lot of historical evidence in the form of inscriptions scattered in Central Java and East Java, as well as building many temples patterned either Hindu or Buddhist. History and cultural heritage the kingdom of Mataram, Indonesia.


910 Sanjaya King Daksa succeeds Balitung in Mataram. He begins building the major Hindu temples at Prambanan.

924 Sanjaya King Wawa takes throne of Mataram, rules until 928.

929 Sanjaya King Mpu Sindok takes power. He moves the court from Mataram to East Java (near Jombang). A major eruption of Mount Merapi in 928 or 929 may have been the reason that the king of Mataram and many of his subjects moved east.

985 Dharmavamsa becomes king of Mataram. He conquers Bali and founds a settlement in western Kalimantan.

1006 Srivijaya attacks and destroys the capital of Mataram. The palace is burned, and Dharmavamsa is killed. Airlangga (then 15 years old) escapes the destruction. Several years of chaos in eastern Java follow.
