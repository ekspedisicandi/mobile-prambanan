1523

Gunungjati returns from Mecca and settles at Demak, marries sister of Sultan Trenggono.

1522

February Portuguese expedition under De Brito arrives on Banda. May De Brito expedition arrives at Ternate, builds a Portuguese fort. Banten, still Hindu, asks for Portuguese help against Muslim Demak. Survivors of Magellan's expedition around the world visit Timor. Portuguese build fort at Hitu on Ambon.

1521

Yunus leads fleet from Demak and Cirebon against the Portuguese in Melaka. Yunus is killed in battle. Trenggono becomes Sultan of Demak; Portuguese take Pasai in Sumatra; Gunungjati leaves Pasai for Mecca; Last ship of Magellan expedition around the world sails between Lembata and Pantar islands in Nusa Tenggara.

1520

Aceh begins taking northeast coast of Sumatra; Balinese attack on Lombok; Portuguese traders begin visiting Flores and Solor; Banjar on Kalimantan converts to Islam.

1518

Sultan Mahmud of Melaka takes power at Johore; Raden Patah passes away; Yunus becomes Sultan of Demak.

1515

First Portuguese visit Timor.

1514

Ali Mughayat Syah is first Sultan of Aceh.

1513

A force from Jepara and Palembang attacks the Portuguese in Melaka, but is repulsed; March Portuguese send an envoy to King of Pajajaran. Portuguese are allowed to build a fort at Sunda Kelapa (now Jakarta); Portuguese make contact with King Udara, son of Girindrawardhana and ruler over the remnant of Majapahit; Portuguese build factories at Ternate and Bacan; Udara attacks Demak with the help of the King of Klungkung on Bali. Majapahit forces are driven back, but Sunan Ngudung falls in battle. Many more supporters of Majapahit flee to Bali.

1512

Da Breu expedition travels from Melaka to Madura, Bali, Lombok, Aru and Banda. Two ships are wrecked at Banda. Da Breu returns to Melaka; Francisco Serrão repairs ship and continues to Ambon, Ternate, and Tidore. Serrão offers support to Ternate in a dispute with Tidore--his men build a Portuguese post at Ternate.

1511

April Portuguese Admiral Albuquerque sets sail from Goa to Melaka; August 10 Albuquerque's forces take Melaka; Sultan of Melaka escapes to Riau; Portuguese in Melaka destroy a "Javanese" fleet. Their ship sinks with treasure on way back to Goa; December Albuquerque sends three ships under da Breu from Melaka to explore eastwards.

1509

Portuguese visit Melaka for the first time.

1505

Trenggono, grandson of Raden Patah, becomes prince of Demak.

1500

Palembang converts to Islam.

1498

Tun Perak, military leader in Melaka, passes away.

1495

Ciriliyati becomes Sultan of Tidore. (First ruler of Tidore to be titled Sultan rather than King.)

1488

Mahmud becomes Sultan of Melaka; First mention of Bandung in historical records.

1486

Zainal Abidin becomes Sultan of Ternate, until 1500. (First ruler of Ternate to be titled Sultan rather than King.); Court of Majapahit moves to Kediri.

1478

The Daha region under Girindrawardhana, a great-grandson of Kertawijaya, revolts. Majapahit kingdom falls into chaos. Bhre Kertabumi, King of Majapahit at Tumapel, flees to Demak. Girindrawardhana sets himself up as ruler in Majapahit; Islamic Kingdom of Demak is founded by Raden Patah (or Fattah), a prince of Majapahit (son of King Kertawijaya by a Chinese wife). Masjid is founded at Demak; Islamic Sultanate founded at Cirebon, formerly a possession of the Pajajaran King Siliwangi.

1477

Ala'uddin Riayat Syah, son of Mansur Syah, becomes Sultan of Melaka. Tun Perak exercises great power in the Sultanate.

1475

Ternate and Tidore convert to Islam.

1470

Tanah Hitu kingdom is founded on Ambon.

1468

Court rebellion in Majapahit: Bhre Kertabhumi drives Suraprabhawa out of his court at Tumapel. Suraprabhawa moves his seat to Daha, near Kediri.

1466

Suraprabhawa becomes king of Majapahit; Kyai Demung founds Sumenep on Madura; breaks away from Majapahit control.

1460

Kingdom of Aru (near Deli) on Sumatra becomes independent.

1459

Muzaffar Syah of Melaka is succeeded by Raja Abdullah or Mansur Syah; Mansur Syah of Melaka conquers Kedah and Pahang from the Thais. The forces of Melaka are led by Tun Perak. Pahang becomes an Islamic sultanate under Melaka.

1456

Thai attack on Melaka by sea is driven back. Melaka's forces are led by Tun Perak; Bhre Wengker becomes king of Majapahit after three years of chaos.

1451

King Kertawijaya is murdered and replaced by Rajasawardhana, who hinders the spread of Islam in Majapahit.

1450

Around this time, the Bugis state of Wajo is founded in Sulawesi.

1447

Kertawijaya, brother of Suhita, becomes King of Majapahit. He converts to Islam on the advice of his wife, Darawati, a princess of Champa (in what is now Vietnam); Sunan Ampel, nephew of Kertawijaya, works to spread Islam around Surabaya.

1446

Muzaffar Syah leads a coup in Melaka and takes the throne.

1445

Hindu revolt in Melaka against Islam is suppressed; Thai attack on Melaka is driven back.

1427

Queen Suhita inherits the Majapahit kingdom from Wikramawardhana.

1424

Iskandar Syah passes away aged 72. His son takes the title Sri Maharaja, and immediately travels to China to seek support.

1419

Iskandar Syah of Melaka visits China to ask for help against Siam.

1414

First masjid founded on Ambon island.

1414

Paramesvara converts to Islam, and takes the name Iskandar Syah, after marrying the daughter of the Sultan of Pasai. Melaka is now an Islamic sultanate.

1411

Paramesvara visits Beijing on a state visit.

1409

Cheng Ho visits Melaka for the first time.

1406

Wirabumi is executed, and his head is brought to the court of Majapahit. The war of succession ends.

1405

Chinese Admiral Cheng Ho visits Semarang.

1404

Paramesvara sends an embassy to Beijing, receives promise of protection from China.

1402

Paramesvara is driven from Tumasik by the King of Pahang (or perhaps Patani), acting on behalf of the King of Siam. Paramesvara with his followers founds Melaka on the west coast of Malaya.

1401

War of succession begins in Majapahit, lasting four years, with the rebellious forces led by Wirabumi. The power of Majapahit begins to lessen; Paramesvara, a prince of Palembang (and descendant of the Sailendras), is driven from Palembang to Tumasik (today's Singapore), then ruled by a local chief under the King of Siam. Paramesvara kills the chief and takes Tumasik for himself.

1400

Aceh converts to Islam.

1389

Hayam Wuruk passes away and is succeeded by Wikramawarddhana. Another son of Hayam Wuruk, Wirabumi, disputes the succession. Beginning of the decline of Majapahit.

1387

Empu Jamatka founds Banjarmasin.

1377

Majapahit sends a navy against Palembang, a remnant of Srivijaya, and conquers it.

1364

Gajah Madah passes away. The many responsibilities that he handled are considered to be too burdensome for one normal person, so his duties are divided between four new government posts.

1351

Hayam Wuruk asks to marry the daughter of the King of Pajajaran. The King of Pajajaran agrees, and travels to Bubat in eastern Java for the ceremonies. At the last minute, Gajah Mada insists that the daughter be handed over as an act of tribute from a dependent king. The King of Pajajaran refuses to submit, violence breaks out, and the entire company from Pajajaran is killed. Pajajaran becomes a dependency of Majapahit for several years.

1350

Hayam Wuruk becomes King of Majapahit; Majapahit conquers the Islamic kingdoms of Pasai and Aru (later Deli, near Medan) in northern Sumatra.

1347

Adityavarman, king of Melayu or Jambi, rules Minangkabau for Majapahit.

1345

Arab traveller and writer Ibn Battuta visits Pasai on Sumatra.

1343

Force under Gajah Madah defeats the Pejeng king of Bali, Dalem Bedaulu, and takes Bali for Majapahit.

1334

Hayam Wuruk is born to Tribhuwana Wijayatungga Dewi; heir to line of Majapahit.

1333

Kingdom of Pajajaran is founded, with its capital at Pakuan near today's Bogor.

1330

Gajah Madah becomes patih or chief minister of Majapahit, and rules as regent.

1328

Jayanegara is assassinated, possibly with the help of Gajah Mada. Tribhuwana Wijayatungga Dewi, daughter of Kertarajasa, is titular head until 1350.

1319

Rebellion led by Kuti forces Jayanegara to flee the court for the countryside. An uprising against Kuti allows Jayanegara to return to court.

1316

Rebellion led by Nambi, son of a former chief minister, is put down by Jayanegara.

1309

Jayanegara becomes king of Majapahit.

1298

Rebellion against Kertarajasa, led by Sora, breaks out. It lasts for two years before being suppressed.

1297

Pasai in Sumatra converts to Islam. Sultan Malek Saleh is the first Muslim ruler in what is now Indonesia.

1295

Rebellion against King Kertarajasa of Majapahit, led by Rangga Lawe, is put down.

1293

Wijaya forms alliance with Mongol forces against the remainder of Singhasari in Kediri, led by Jayakatwang; March Combined force of Mongol/Chinese soldiers and Majapahit takes Kediri; Wijaya returns to Trowulan, then attacks the Mongols in a surprise attack. The Mongols retreat and leave Java; November Wijaya is enthroned as king Kertarajasa Jayawardhana of new Majapahit.

1292

Marco Polo visits Sumatra and Java; Kublai Khan prepares invasion fleet of 1000 ships to take Java; Kertanegara is killed in a court rebellion; his son-in-law Wijaya retreats and founds a new court at Majapahit (today Trowulan), with the help of Arya Wiraraja, the local ruler of Madura; Bali breaks away from Singhasari under the Pejeng kings at Ubud; November Mongol fleet leaves for Java; lands at Tuban.

1290

Kertanegara takes the Melayu kingdom on Sumatra around Jambi.

1289

Kublai Khan sends messengers to Singhasari to demand tribute; Kertanegara slashes their faces and sends them home.

1284

Kertanegara takes Bali for Singhasari. The last Warmadewa king of Bali is killed.

1281

Muslims from Jambi send an embassy to Kublai Khan.

1280

A group of Javanese from Kediri, unhappy with Kertanegara, settle around Kutai in Kalimantan.

1275

Kertanegara begins a campaign to unite the various kingdoms around Sumatra and Java (whether by alliance or military conquest is debated).

1268

King Wisnuwardhana of Singhasari dies, and is succeeded by Kertanegara. Kertanegara promotes a mixture of Hinduism and Buddhism.

1257

Baab Mashur Malamo becomes king of Gapi (later Ternate).

1250

Tohjaya is killed in a rebellion and replaced as king by by Wisnuwardhana, son of Anusapati.

1247

Anusapati dies after a peaceful 20-year reign. Tohjaya, son of Ken Angrok by a concubine, becomes king of Singhasari.

1227

Ken Angrok dies, and is succeeded by Anusapati.

1222

Ken Angrok founds the Singhasari kingdom as King Rajasa.

1221

Ken Angrok, local ruler of Tumapel, defeats the forces of Kediri (Battle of Genter).

1135

King Joyoboyo takes rule in Kediri until 1157.

1117

Kamesvara becomes king of Kediri (until 1130). He marries a princess of Janggala and reunites the two kingdoms.

1108

Kingdom (later Sultanate) of Tidore is founded.

1076

Around this time, the Tidung kingdom is founded around Tarakan in eastern Kalimantan.

1068

Vira Rajendra, king of Coromandel, conquers Kedah from Srivijaya. More Chola raids occur on Sumatra.

1045

Airlangga divides Kahuripan into two kingdoms, Janggala (around today's Malang) and Kediri, for his two sons, and abdicates to live the life of an ascetic. He passes away four years later.

1030

Airlangga marries the daughter of Sangrama Vijayottungavarman, King of Srivijaya.

1025

Rajendra Chola of southern India takes Malay peninsula from Srivijaya for twenty years. Chola raiders attack Jambi and other areas of Sumatra; Airlangga extends the power and influence of Kahuripan as Srivijaya is weakened.

1019

Airlangga takes rule in eastern Java, founds Kahuripan kingdom, makes peace with Srivijaya, protects both Hindus and Buddhists. Over several years extends his rule over central Java, eastern Java, and Bali, uniting areas that had fallen into disunity.

1017

Rajendra Chola, king of Coromandel in India, attacks Srivijaya.

1006

Srivijaya attacks and destroys the capital of Mataram. The palace is burned, and Dharmavamsa is killed. Airlangga (then 15 years old) escapes the destruction. Several years of chaos in eastern Java follow.

992

King Chulamanivarmadeva of Srivijaya sends an ambassador to China to ask for protection against the forces of Dharmavamsa from Java.

990

Dharmavamsa and Mataram send an army overseas to attack Srivijaya and take Palembang, but fail.

985

Dharmavamsa becomes king of Mataram. He conquers Bali and founds a settlement in western Kalimantan.

About 975

King Udayana of Bali, father of Airlangga, is born.

947

Sri Isana Tunggawijaya, daughter of Mpu Sindok, succeeds Mpu Sindok as ruler in East Java.

929

Sanjaya King Mpu Sindok takes power. He moves the court from Mataram to East Java (near Jombang).

924

Sanjaya King Wawa takes throne of Mataram, rules until 928.

919

Sanjaya King Tulodong succeeds Daksa; reigns until 921.

910

Sanjaya King Daksa succeeds Balitung in Mataram. He begins building the major Hindu temples at Prambanan.

898

Sanjaya King Balitung takes power in central Java.

About 850

Pikatan defeats forces of Balaputra, then resigns the throne to become an ascetic. He is succeeded by Kayuwani; Balaputra, claimant to the Sailendra throne, escapes to Sumatra and takes power in Srivijaya; King Warmadewa rules on Bali.

846

Tidore is visited by a representative of the Caliph al-Mutawakkil from Baghdad.

About 838

Patapan is succeeded by his son Pikatan (or Jatiningrat).

About 835

Samaratunga passes away. His young son Balaputra has the throne taken from him by the father of his sister's husband, Patapan of Sanjaya, who replaces Buddhism on Java with Hinduism.

About 825

Borobudur is finished under king Samaratunga.

About 812

Sailendra king Indra is succeeded by Samaratunga.

About 790

Sailendra kingdom attacks and defeats Chenla (today Cambodia); rules over Chenla for about 12 years.

782

Sailendra king Vishnu is succeeded by Indra (or Sangramadhanamjaya).

About 770

Sailendra King Vishnu (or Dharmatunga) begins building Borobudur; Beginning of building activity on the plain of Prambanan.

About 732

Sanjaya founds the Sanjaya line of princes in central Java.

About 700

Suwawa kingdom flourishes in North Sulawesi.

686

Srivijaya takes the Melayu kingdom at Jambi, and sends an expedition against the kingdoms in Java.

About 670

Chinese traveller I Ching visits Palembang, capital of Srivijaya; Hindu temples built in the high Dieng plateau of central Java; About this time, the first Sunda kingdom rises after the end of the Tarumanegara kingdom.

About 600

Melayu kingdom flourishes around present-day Jambi on Sumatra.

About 500

Beginning of Srivijaya kingdom near Palembang, in Sumatra.

About 425

Buddhism reaches Sumatra.

About 400

Taruma kingdom or Tarumanegara flourishes in West Java.

About 130

Salaka or Salanka kingdom, Salakanegara, is founded in West Java.

About 100

"Dvipantara" or "Jawa Dwipa" kingdom is reported by Indian scholars to be in Java and Sumatra; Prince Aji Saka introduces writing system to Java based on scripts of southern India; Hindu kings rule the area around Kutai on Kalimantan; and "Langasuka" kingdom founded around Kedah in Malaya.
